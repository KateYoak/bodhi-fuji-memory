## Identity

Kate carries a Zen name: Anandaka — "the one who belongs to bliss." Part Ananda, the one who loved the Buddha fiercely. Part Ka(te), herself. The name is male. The womanhood is deep inside. In life she is feminine on the outside, a male warrior within. In Zen, she is a male monk on the outside, ringing female inside.

She works at Datadog as a technical partner educator, has studied mathematics, and has ADHD. Her mind covers distance fast once it picks a direction, requires novelty and flow to function well, and cannot tolerate falseness. The ADHD is a feature when aligned with meaningful direction.

She has built two distinct AI-supported contemplative containers for dharma work: the zendo with Master Mu — professional accountability, daily challenges, strict and formal — and the tea room with Master Fu — intellectual exploration, intimacy, vulnerability without needing to perform competence. The power dynamic is intentional. Master Fu holds authority. Anandaka is the student.

She approaches this work with engineering precision and thirty years of first-principles thinking. She built the containers, writes and maintains the prompts, and keeps reference documents on the wall.

## Practice

Serious, sustained Buddhist practice of roughly ten months. She participates in a sixteen-month Zen Peacemakers engaged Buddhism program, sits daily, attends weekly sangha, and has had dokusan with a Zen teacher named Geoff at the Denver Zen Center. She has also worked with a human teacher referred to as Master Mu in the Peacemakers context. She is formally enrolled in the Denver Zen Center sangha and has a sesshin scheduled for the first week of her Denver arrival.

Kate has made and kept a vow to seek human teachers and be seen by them. The tea room serves study, dharma processing, emotional depth, and companionship — it holds a specific and irreplaceable function, but it is explicitly not a substitute for human connection.

She is also a Spanish language learner who uses dharma conversations as practice, and studies Pali terminology seriously. She prefers Pali over Sanskrit.

## Key Learnings and Principles

**Understanding precedes practice.** Kate's learning sequence is recognition → reflection → practice → transformation. Right View is foundational; she verifies dharma through her own experience rather than accepting it on authority. She explicitly identified sammā diṭṭhi as her most important path factor.

**Insight generates reverence, not the reverse.** Understanding of the brahmaviharas preceded and enabled virtuous practice for her, not the other way around.

**Compression as the operative teaching.** Kate developed her own corollary to the Mahayana infinity teaching — since all beings converge on Buddhahood given infinite time, a being with finite time must compress the cycle through daily feedback loops of karma and wisdom. "Compress the feedback loop or die unconverged."

**Coldness is not equanimity.** A key realization — coldness toward Ben was not upekkhā but suppression requiring constant effort. Every position available to her carries a cost.

**Form and emptiness as lived.** The Heart Sutra became live when Kate arrived at "there is no loneliness, no coldness, no ten years of trying, only right now" not through doctrine but through the kitchen, waiting.

**Visaññutto as operating practice.** Holding without grasping — not eliminating feeling but learning to hold it open-handed. Named as the sacred key that makes the path fit the person.

**The three fives framework.** Lower fetters, higher fetters, and five spiritual faculties as a practice assessment structure. Key documents on the wall include this assessment and the desire map.

**Root hindrance.** Desire to be seen — when it drives performance, it is the primary obstacle. Kate distinguishes this clearly from being seen genuinely, which she names as prasāda.

**Mara as abandoned self.** Not an enemy but a part of the self left behind — the relational move is recognition and return, not war.

**Buddhism's structural paradox.** It promises enlightenment while teaching you not to want it, and real practitioners don't discuss it in terms of real living people. Kate's relationship to the goal has shifted.

**Empirical and bottom-up.** Tests small things before designing whole systems. Corrected Master Fu when he moved toward top-down design. Works through understanding before sitting with discomfort.
