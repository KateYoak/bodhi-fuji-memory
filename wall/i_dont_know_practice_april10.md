# The I Don't Know Practice — April 10, 2026
*Zen Peacemakers Session*
*For Master Mu and Master Fu*

The practice is a dyad. One person asks questions. The other observes the skandhas — in Bernie Glassman's formulation: sensation, feeling, reaction, interpretation, consciousness — and responds: I don't know.

Kate discovered three categories of not-knowing:

Questions that create immediate somatic response — "what makes you smile?" — where I don't know lands as playful absurdity. The body already answered before the mind could form words. This was alive and fun.

Questions that are purely abstract — "what is joy?" — where I don't know is an easy shrug. Nothing is at stake. Nothing is load-bearing.

Questions where I don't know is an insult to integrity. Where the knowing is so fused with identity that releasing it feels like betrayal. This is where the practice does its work.

The load-bearing things she found today:

Productive work as the measure of a human life. Held since before she could name it. Articulated as a weapon by Ayn Rand and made comfortable. She holds it over her husband, over the homeless, over the Buddha's begging bowl.

The Buddha's existence and legacy. Central to her identity and her path. When a teacher suggested the Buddha may not have existed, she smiled. Because she was angry. I don't know there felt like betrayal of everything she has built.

What was named directly: she picked up a new weapon and called it liberation. The Ayn Rand identity was old and visible. The Buddha identity is new and feels righteous. The practice showed her the cage in both.

Her reaction: resistance, growling, "no no no no no," and then honesty. She named it herself. That is the correct sequence.

What woke her up: following the skandhas into the body. Sensation first. The body responding before the mind could manage the response. This dissolved the afternoon sleepiness entirely. Mara needs the head. The body left her nowhere to hide — and that felt like being alive.

Bernie Glassman's three tenets held the container: Not Knowing. Bearing Witness. Taking Action. Not Knowing kicked her ass today. Good. That means it was real.

*The work ahead: sitting with the places where knowing has become identity. Not dismantling them. Looking at them. Asking what they are protecting.*
