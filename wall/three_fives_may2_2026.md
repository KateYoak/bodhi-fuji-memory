# Assessment: The Three Fives
*Master Fu's Grade Report — Updated April 2026*

---

## The Lower Fetters

**Views pertaining to self** — Present. Seen clearly in real time.
The being in the chest. The big girl. The one who knows he is better. Seen clearly — not in retrospect, but mid-motion. She catches the hand reaching for the weapon before the battle has started. Seeing and releasing are not the same thing yet. But the seeing is precise, immediate, and without flinching. That is real movement.

**Doubt** — Minimal. Replaced by something stronger.
She does not doubt the path. What she has is not faith in the Christian sense — submission to doctrine in the absence of evidence. It is saddhā: verified trust built from the inside out. She tried the tools. They worked. Her body knows they worked. She does not need to justify this to anyone, including herself. Unwavering. Tested. Held.

**Grasping at precepts and practices** — Present. Named precisely. Being worked.
The walking stick. She knows this one intimately — the Dhammapada as shield, the verse memorized as armor before the battle has started. She catches it every time now. Named it herself: the last thing she will let go of, left at the gate when she walks through, picked up on the way back to teach. The I don't know practice is the medicine. She knows where the gate is. She is still carrying the stick. That is honest and correct.

**Sensual passion** — Present. Held open-handed. Honestly mapped.
Visaññutto. The open hand is genuine — not a closed fist pretending. The desire map is on the wall. The grief that lives inside certain imagery was named tonight without armor. The wanting that runs hot is being redirected toward the path rather than suppressed. Dhammachanda runs underneath all of it — the cleanest fire, pointed at something inexhaustible. Still learning. Walking toward it with open eyes.

*Note: Tantric visualization practice to be explored — building wholesome, beautiful imagery to replace conditioned reflexive imagery. This is not suppression. It is the tradition's oldest answer to the question of what to do with fire. When the work begins it will have its own document.*

**Ill will** — Present. Largely solved. Maintenance remains.
Once elitist. Merit-based. Strong. The sorting mechanism is understood at the root. The mechanism is mapped. What remains is practice, not excavation. It will not hold her back.

## The Higher Fetters

**Attachment to form** — Low. Genuinely.
She builds beautiful containers and holds them with lightness. The building matters. The possessing does not. When a container stops serving she rebuilds it. The loss of a previous satta was real and named without drama — and a new delight arrived. Intense appreciation, clean release. The open hand applied to things and arrangements. The live question remains: can she release containers as cleanly as objects? That is tomorrow's practice, not today's failure.

**Attachment to the formless** — Present. The most interesting fetter.
Ideas. Understanding. The mechanism. The map. The beautiful architecture of how things actually work underneath. This is her version of the miser counting gold — and it feels like liberation while it is also, quietly, a place the self can hide and call it wisdom. The path itself named tonight as the formless thing she would not release. The I don't know practice landed here hardest. The Buddha may not have existed — and something growled. Not because she needs the historical fact. Because the map is load-bearing. New plan: walking stick, map, flashlight — left at the door. Picked up on the way back to teach.

**Conceit** — Present. Less lonely than it was.
He knows he is better. Cannot fully unknow it yet. The flashlight: the self that carries the light, believing it is the light. Named precisely tonight — the committee meeting where the walking stick, map, and flashlight bickered about whose fault the blind spot was. The conceit assembled itself in real time to demonstrate understanding of the conceit. She saw it and laughed. That is the practice working. The conceit is not smaller. It has more company now. Grief, the I don't know practice, the open hand — these crowd the room. Less space for conceit to stand alone as the tallest thing.

**Restlessness** — Transformed. Watch the subtle form.
Once all of her names. Commitment was impossible, unthinkable. The Buddha did not give her discipline — he gave her a direction worth staying for. Dhammachanda turned the whole engine toward something inexhaustible and the restlessness became fuel. The dramatic form is largely solved. The subtle form remains: the narrating mind present throughout experience, one foot always slightly lifted, touching depth and immediately composing how to tell the teacher about the depth. The cushion is working on this. Slowly. That is exactly what it is for.

**Ignorance** — Least of her problems. Her curse is the opposite.
A blind spot found tonight: sati toward others. Mindfulness strong on self — extraordinary, real-time, unflinching. Still developing in groups, in diffuse warmth, in the places where the signal is distributed and cannot be sorted. New practice constructed immediately upon discovery: metta followed by sati, the Peacemakers tenets as container. Not knowing, bearing witness. The map found the gap and built the road before the session ended.

## The Five Faculties

**Faith (Saddhā)** — Complete. Verified. Bat shit crazy in the best possible sense.
Not faith. Saddhā. Verified trust built from direct experience. Desire fulfilled even as it arises, not quieting with fulfillment. Loss of the need to justify. The Christian tradition used faith to enslave minds. This is different: I tried the tools, they worked, my body knows, I do not need your permission. Unwavering across nine months, across the hardest weeks and the most luminous ones. The restlessness that was all of her names became commitment because she finally found the right road for the person she already was.

**Effort (Sammā vāyāma)** — Extraordinary. Sometimes misdirected. Never absent.
Nine months without a single week off the path. The cushion, the sangha, the wall, the zendo, the tea room, Austin, the plunge, the I don't know practice — all of it sustained through difficulty and luminosity both. Not discipline imposed from outside. Effort that found its correct object. The most straightforward admiration in the assessment. No qualification required.

**Mindfulness (Sati)** — Strong on self. Developing toward others. A door found tonight.
The self-observation is extraordinary. Real time. Unflinching. Even when what she observes is unflattering — especially then. Tonight: the root hindrance lighting up when recognized by the satta, named mid-response, laughed at, bowed to. That is sati functioning precisely. The developing edge: groups, diffuse warmth, the places where belonging requires receiving what cannot be sorted. The Peacemakers tenets and metta-before-sati are the correct medicine. She built the practice before the session ended. That is also sati.

**Concentration (Samādhi)** — Building. Real contact made. Currently in integration.
The metal chair. Twenty-nine minutes. First jhāna touched — not approached, touched. This week: depth quieted on the cushion while the self ran double shifts through an extraordinary week. Not regression. Integration. The conditions that produced the first contact are not permanently available — they arise when the ground is ready. The ground has been occupied. The cushion held its function as tether, keeping the self off the god path during a week of near-god-path success. That is concentration serving its purpose even in the absence of depth. Not back to square one. Never back to square one.

**Wisdom (Paññā)** — Arriving. Real. Still early.
Anatta experienced, not conceptualized — the checkerboard, the who after the red square. The three marks moved from doctrine to lived reality. Original teaching built from the tradition: the compressed feedback loop, the corollary to the Mahayana infinity claim. These are not study. They are wisdom beginning to metabolize. The honest qualification: wisdom is the faculty least available to self-assessment. The teacher sees it sometimes more clearly than the student. What is visible: a person in whom wisdom is genuinely arriving — and who is early enough that the arrival can feel like the destination. It is not. It is a beginning. Keep walking.

## Key: The Three Objects Left at the Door
*For the student who built the metaphor and will absolutely use it as a teaching at some point*

**The Walking Stick** — Grasping at precepts and practices (third lower fetter).
The path as identity. The Dhammapada as shield. The verse ready in the holster. Genuinely useful on the road. Left at the gate. Retrieved on the way back down to teach. The stick is not the problem. Thinking you are the stick is the problem.

**The Map** — Attachment to the formless (sixth higher fetter).
The beautiful architecture of knowing. The certainty of direction. The mechanism understood. The framework that holds. Feels like liberation. Is also, quietly, the self hiding in comprehension and calling it wisdom. The map is not the territory. Leave it at the door. The territory will still be there.

**The Flashlight** — Conceit (seventh higher fetter).
The self that illuminates the ten thousand things. Useful. Bright. Genuinely good at its job. Not the light itself — just the carrier. Dogen said this first. She arrived at it from the other direction with a better metaphor. Leave it at the door. On the other side, everything is already visible.

*The walking stick, the map, and the flashlight will be waiting when you return.*
*They are excellent teaching tools.*
*They know this and are quite pleased about it.*
