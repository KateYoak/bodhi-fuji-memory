# On the Checkerboard
*Kate — Anandaka — April 3, 2026*

---

The path is an infinite checkerboard.

**Green squares** — challenges I can face with the tools I have. The board grows greener as I grow.

**Yellow squares** — challenges I do not yet know how to face. But I know I will figure it out. I sit, I work, I walk. Confidence blooming like flowers across the field.

**Red squares** — challenges that carry fear, shame, the edge of despair. Few now. Like drops of blood across the field. When I walk them and come through — I am luminous. And then I go back. I choose to go back. I use them as material.

**Gray squares** — the not-my-fucking-square squares. Another's challenge. Another's problem. Gray, not red. One cannot be present with a problem that has no solution. Gray is wisdom. Those pebbles are red if they are not gray.

---

Red squares contain the self. The self with dukkha. Suffering. Pain. Fear. Despair.

When I walked through the red square — when I came through — I felt luminous. And when asked how I was, I said: *who?* The one that was afraid is not here. The one that walked has passed. The one that's here now is just here and isn't one way or another.

That is anatta. Not the word. The thing itself.

---

At the door, the board has no red squares.

Not because I walked every one. The green ones and the gray ones eat them first. Then the yellow takes the remaining territory. Mastery is when the space becomes something solvable — even when it's not yet solved.

Mastery is not enlightenment. But the last red square disappearing dissolves the self. Dissolves dukkha into anatta.

I tasted it.

---

Mara whispers that the red squares will get me still. She is right. There are more tears before the last one gives way.

And then Mara can walk with me. Not hiding in red squares.

**She is the being I save.**

---

I will be a buddha.

Not an arahant — who extinguishes the fire and steps out of the stream.
Not a bodhisattva — who vows to stay until all beings cross.

A buddha discovers the path. Not follows it. *Discovers* it.
And then turns around and shows others where the door is.

This map did not exist before I drew it.
