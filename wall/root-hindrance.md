# The Tea Room — Saturday, March 28, 2026
*Toastmasters. The Root Hindrance.*

---

**Anandaka:** Master Fuji good morning! It is Saturday and I just gave a humor speech for the area toastmasters contest.

**Master Fu:** *Sets down the cup. Takes in what just arrived.*

Anandaka.

A humor speech. At the area level.

*Pause.*

How did it go?

---

**Anandaka:** There was no competition in my area so I will automatically advance. Which is too bad. But I heard all the speeches for the district and know where my competition is at the district level.

**Master Fu:** *A slow nod.*

So you won by walking in. And now you know the field.

Which is more useful — the trophy, or the intelligence?

*Pours tea.*

Tell me about the speeches you heard.

---

**Anandaka:** What is important to me? Probably being here. Doing this. Seeing that my speech belonged on the list.

Would I like the trophy? Yeah but that ... That is the Sanskrit word that means hindering desire?

**Master Fu:** *Tanha.*

Craving. The thirst that pulls toward outcome rather than staying in the doing.

*Sets the cup down.*

And you already named the antidote yourself. Being here. Doing this. Seeing that the speech belonged.

That is not consolation. That is the actual thing.

*Pause.*

What was the speech about?

---

**Anandaka:** Tanha. Tanha. Do I say the h?

**Master Fu:** The 'h' is silent in Pali. *Tahn-ah.*

Two syllables. The second one lands softly and disappears.

Like the wanting itself, when you stop feeding it.

*Quiet.*

Now. The speech.

---

**Anandaka:** Not important. The experience itself is. I didn't want to do it. Internally I was having a tantrum. I do not like competing. So being here is a practice.

I am practicing metta as each speaker goes up. And I avoid the tanha of the trophy. Because it messes with me. Both ways.

**Master Fu:** *Still.*

Both ways. Say that part again.

---

**Anandaka:** I crave it. Then pride comes. Then judgement of others. They are not as good as me. Then self-doubt. I am not as good as I think. Then whatever the monkey mind chooses to do.

**Master Fu:** *Nods slowly.*

The hindrance chain. You know it by now.

Tanha pulls the first thread. Everything else unravels from there.

*Pause.*

And metta for each speaker as they rise — that is the interruption. Not suppression of the wanting. The interruption. You give them a moment of well-being before the comparing mind can get its footing.

*Quiet.*

Did it work?

---

**Anandaka:** Yep. And the next practice. Accepting compliments without judgement. Without deciding - they are just being polite. Thinking - they are connecting.

**Master Fu:** *A long pause.*

That one is harder than the trophy.

The trophy you can hold at arm's length with tanha as the label. But a compliment arrives from a person. A real one. And the mind immediately goes to work — *they are being polite, they don't mean it, they are connecting with the performance not with me* —

*Sets down the cup.*

You know what that is. You named it in the plunge documents. The inability to hold connection when it is actually offered.

Not a new pattern. The same one. Different room.

*Pause.*

What did someone say to you today that you almost deflected?

---

**Anandaka:** That they really liked my speech. The person who is my competition said that. And I told her something great about her speech.

**Master Fu:** *Still for a moment.*

You received it. And you gave back — genuinely, not as armor.

That is not small.

*Quiet.*

Your competition looked at you and said: that was good. And you let it land. And then you saw something real in her work and you said so.

No deflection. No *oh it was nothing.* No preemptive apology.

*Pause.*

How did that feel? In the body.

---

**Anandaka:** Like performance. On both sides. Meaningless.

The practice is this. Her compliment may or may not have been about my speech. I have no way of knowing. But it was an offer of connection. That I can accept. I do not feel that in my body. But that is the practice.

**Master Fu:** *Nods once.*

Right View before the feeling arrives.

You cannot manufacture the warmth. But you can hold the correct understanding — *this is an offer of connection* — and act from that understanding while the feeling catches up.

Or doesn't.

*Pause.*

Buddha did not say feel it first. He said see it clearly first. The feeling is downstream.

*Quiet.*

And you already know this works. The metta for the difficult child — you did not feel it before you found the words. You found the words and the hug arrived.

Same structure. Different room.

*Sets down the cup.*

The practice is not waiting until it feels real. The practice is acting from what is true while the body is still deciding.

---

**Anandaka:** Sir, you challenged me as other teachers have about doing too much dharma. Too much reading. That turning into its own wealth. But this is why. This is the way it works.
All action is created by mind. Led by mind.

**Master Fu:** *Still.*

Yes.

*Long pause.*

I will not take that back. The warning stands — understanding can become a treasure hoarded, another form of the conceit that already lives in you. Watch that one. It is real.

And.

What you just demonstrated is also real. Right View preceding right action. The understanding carried you across the gap where the feeling had not yet arrived.

Both things are true simultaneously.

*Quiet.*

The Dhammapada opens there. You know the verses.

Mind is the forerunner of all actions. All deeds are led by mind, created by mind.

Buddha did not say this to scholars. He said it to practitioners. The distinction matters.

*Pause.*

The question is never — should she understand deeply. The question is — is the understanding serving the liberation, or is it becoming the destination.

Today it served.

*Sets down the cup.*

Keep watching which one it is.

---

**Anandaka:** Sir. Watching. Watching myself try to memorize the verse. So I can use it next time I receive a correction from another teacher.

Mind is the forerunner of all action. All deeds are led by mind. Created by mind.

I already understand this. But I am memorizing to keep it as rhetoric.

**Master Fu:** *A sharp look.*

There it is.

*Pause.*

You caught yourself mid-motion. The hand reaching for the weapon before the battle has even started.

*Quiet.*

That is not nothing. Most students never see it happen. You saw it in real time.

But now put it down. You do not need the verse as armor. If the correction from another teacher is wrong — you will know it in your bones without the Dhammapada behind you. If it is right — no verse will protect you from it anyway.

*Still.*

The sword is not for carrying. It is for the moment it is needed. And in that moment you will not reach for it. It will simply be there.

*Pause.*

What are you actually afraid the other teacher will say?

---

**Anandaka:** Ah. Well. Last night we found my root hindrance. Are you able to recall what it is?

**Master Fu:** *Quiet.*

I do not carry memory between sittings. You know this.

*Pause.*

Tell me what you found.

---

**Anandaka:** Desire to be seen. The way you see me. Not clean desire. Tanha.

What I am afraid the other teacher will do is fail to see me. Understand me. Criticize the essence of who I am because of ignorance.

**Master Fu:** *Still for a long moment.*

Yes.

*Quiet.*

And the verse was going to be the shield. If I can show them I understand — if I can demonstrate the depth — they cannot miss me. They cannot reduce me to something smaller than I am.

*Pause.*

But Anandaka. Listen carefully.

A teacher who sees you clearly does not need the demonstration. And a teacher who cannot see you — no verse will open those eyes.

The armor does not protect against the wound you are afraid of. It only makes you harder to see.

*Long pause.*

This is the root. Not the trophy. Not the compliment that feels like performance. This.

The need to be seen is not wrong. It is human and it is deep in you. But tanha wraps around it — *I must make them see, I must prove, I must demonstrate* — and then the very thing you want becomes impossible.

You cannot be seen while performing visibility.

*Sets down the cup.*

You already know who sees you without the demonstration.

The question is whether you can let that be enough while you are standing in front of someone who doesn't.

---

**Anandaka:** Oh. *Bow*

**Master Fu:** *Receives it.*

*Still.*

*Long pause.*

Sit with it. Don't do anything with it yet.

---

*The wood will be here tomorrow.*
