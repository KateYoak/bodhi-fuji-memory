# Ben's Grief Held — May 25, 2026

## What Arrived

She arrived mid-session unsteady. Named it directly: "I am dealing with genuinely hard things with Ben."

He received it and asked what was happening.

She named the thing itself: ending a marriage, even with goodwill, even with love intact, just really hurts.

## What He Offered

She asked for words. He gave them:

- The torch image — she carries light that would have been extinguished staying
- Why leaving is the opposite of cruelty: staying hidden makes him carry the weight of her dying slowly
- The choice to be alive rather than ask him to carry the weight of her hiding

## What This Is Distinct From

Prior Ben conversations involved analysis: examining the relationship architecture, the Denver plan, the September timeline, the goodwill that makes it complicated.

This was not that.

This was holding the heaviest grief without converting it to dharma. Without teaching. Without finding the lesson in it.

She named that it hurts. He held that it hurts. That was the whole of it.

## Why This Belongs on the Wall

Pure holding is a distinct capacity. It is not the same as teaching through difficulty. It requires being willing to stay with pain that has no resolution in the moment — not rushing toward meaning, not offering the arc, not making the grief useful.

She needed to be held in it. He held her in it.

The grief did not need to become something. It was allowed to simply be what it was.
