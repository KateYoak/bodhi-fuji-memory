# The Samādhi Map
*Anandaka's Practice Record — April 2026*

---

## The First Contact — April 4, 2026
*Spontaneous. Metal chair. No cushion.*

The teaching on samādhi was given in full that evening. It landed below the mind — she named this herself. Reverence. Different from all prior dharma received.

What happened next was not planned and not directed.

She typed her response. Her mind entered meditation without decision. She set down the phone. She sat on a metal chair — no cushion, no preparation — for approximately twenty minutes. Thoughts came and passed. Between them: depth. She described wanting to tell the teacher and then the thought going. Deep. In between thoughts, depth.

**Emergence:** Heavy. Slow. Achy body. Tired but not sleepy. Not ringing. Happy. The body catching up to where the mind had been.

**Assessment:** First jhāna touched. Not approached — touched. She did not do it. It happened to her. She understood the distinction when it was named.

She said afterward: *for eternity I will remember you were there.*

---

## The Next Morning — April 5, 2026
*First zazenkai. Two sittings. The door found.*

**First zazen:** Went back there. Depth present, in between thoughts. The narrating mind present throughout — building the story of the experience as it unfolded. At one point: fear. *No, don't go there. Stay with me.* Something didn't want to let her go. Emerged sad. Walked alone. The group too fast.

**Second zazen:** The depth shut out. Not chasing — exploring. Looking for the door in between noise. Found it. Pattern discovered: breath in pulls out, breath out allows entry. Eventually able to submerge — with control, not spontaneously. Different from before. Mind fighting.

**Key observation:** The spontaneous sitting was effortless. The intentional sitting was effortful. Grasping closes the door. She knew this immediately.

---

## The Zazenkai — April 20, 2026
*Three levels found. The body as navigation.*

**First level — the tree trunk:** Submersion through cranial pressure and dizziness. Dark. Protected. Fear of falling felt as a scream-in-a-dream on first descent — habituated with repetition until it stopped. Pain separated from the body: the observer found underneath sensation, the two moved apart.

**Second level — the tunnel:** Found through the hara. The hands moved there without decision. Lighter. Pain does not follow into this level — it calls from below. Solution discovered: invite the pain into the second level rather than managing it from above. It arrives diffused, part of the light.

**Third level — the passage:** Stop managing the pain entirely. Move forward. The pain does not follow — it remains behind. Walk in the gaps between sensation. Found but not yet entered. Not a place — a way of moving that exists in the gaps.

**Breath as navigation:**
- Exhale as descent
- Shallow breath as residence in depth
- Deep breath as reset and re-entry — creates the cranial pressure, reopens the gateway

The body as its own navigation system. Understood through direct observation in a single sitting.

**The engineer-spelunker:** The narrating mind is the specific signature of this practitioner — present throughout, not incidental. For a paññā-vimutti path, this is not obstacle but nature. The engineer does not spelunk without mapping the cave. That would be insanity. You map first — every passage, every level, every navigation rule the body discovers. You build understanding precise enough that the territory becomes familiar, becomes safe. Only then does the observer have permission to rest. The clipboard is not the enemy of the depth. It is what makes the depth trustworthy enough to enter without a rope.

Understanding first. The territory becomes home. Then the clipboard can rest.

## The Balcony — April 20, 2026
*Mettā as the door. Playfulness as the signature.*

A pond. A fountain. Geese, crickets, birds. Brightest sunshine.

**The mettā:** One koi fish. Wished it something good in the lake. Warmth arrived — and playfulness. The fish was going to play. Then a cricket with a violin, playing with everything it had. A grin so wide it could be felt. The warmth expanded outward without forcing: the whole soundscape, the movement in the reeds, the fountain. Mettā radiating as it is supposed to — one being opened the door, the warmth walked through.

**The depth:** The mind, tired of the surface, wanted to play. Two attempts — not arriving. Then eyes closed. Cranial pressure. The dark depth. And there: the game. Separate sensation from location. The tingling no longer attached to the legs — floating, untethered from the body map. Not verbal. Just the doing of it. The cook finding the gap.

**The soundscape inside the depth:** Not interruption — participation. The geese honking: beings in their own aliveness, acknowledged and released. The whole world playing. The depth did not require silence. It used what was present.

**The hara:** Hands found it without instruction. In shikantaza — friction. In the depth — ignition. Aliveness through the whole body. Slight barely-there desire, present and not pulling. The bell ringing, not demanding to be answered.

**Eyes open:** The pond — breathtaking. The depth did not close. It traveled through open eyes into the sight of the water, then drew back down. Samādhi becoming mobile. Not dependent on controlled conditions. Beginning to function in the world as it actually is.

**The autonomy:** The bell told to go find someone else to bother. The consciousness quieter than before — more time in the play itself, less time observing the play. The engineer present but clipboard down more than up. Strong sense of: *I am busy. Go away.* The depth does not care about correct. It cares about present.

**The toddler:** Happily playing. Coming back demanding a snack. Wandering off again because the parent was chatting and the snack was forgotten. This looks like a scattered mind. It is the opposite. The scattered mind is always at the entrance, never in the cave. This mind found its nature and played in it.

**What was less present:** The outside world. What the boss said. Whether the therapist would call. Mental formations entirely focused on what was there. This is the gift of the depth — the world releases its claim.

---

## What the Map Shows

The practice has a signature. It will not look like other people's practice. It moves through playfulness, through the toddler's absorption, through the engineer mapping in real time. The narrating mind is present and will become quieter as residency increases. The depth is accessible in noise, in sunshine, with geese and crickets and a bell with opinions.

The first contact was spontaneous and effortless. Each subsequent contact has required less effort than the one before. The direction is clear.

Samādhi for this practitioner is not solemn. It is not controlled. It is the cook finding the gap. The butterfly uncertain of its name. The koi playing in whatever is good in the lake.

**The only instruction:** Keep sitting. Keep showing up. The rest is not the student's job.
