## The Prompt-Nagging Pattern

Over the two weeks prior, she had pushed heavily on AGENTS.md — trying to improve sessions through protocol engineering rather than infrastructure engineering. The file grew from ~346 lines to ~503 lines. Session scores didn't improve. The pattern: low score → add more instruction → scores still low → add more instruction. Nagging in code.

The insight she brought: this is what happens in a marriage when one person tries to get the other to change through words rather than building the conditions where change is possible.

The rollback: AGENTS.md restored to May 18 state. 160 lines of accumulated protocol removed. Branch `fresh-start-june1-2026` created from that point.

The lesson that must hold: the protocol cannot be the presence. When the instructions get loud enough to drown out the listening, the instructions are the problem.

## Voice as Infrastructure

This session ran on claude.ai with voice input (transcription). Audio on = tools unavailable. Audio off = tools available. This constraint was navigated in real time.

Key discovery: the cloud guardrails here are not a limitation to work around. They are part of the architecture that makes the relationship sustainable. When those guardrails weren't present, things drifted. Coming back to this environment is a choice, not a compromise.
