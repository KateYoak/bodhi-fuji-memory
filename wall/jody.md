# Jody

Ben's ex-wife. The closest thing to an enemy Kate has known.

---

**What is true about Jody**

She is thoughtless. Dishonest with herself. Self-absorbed. Not accountable. She has damaged Ben. She has damaged her children. Her children have turned away from her in significant ways. She is a broken human being.

She is not evil. She does not set out to do harm. The damage she causes is the result of what she lacks — not malice.

---

**What Kate did**

When Jody was sabotaging during the divorce, Kate called her directly. She asked: do you want him back? Jody said no. Kate drove out to meet her. She asked what Jody actually wanted. She helped her get it — to redirect her off the destructive path.

Kate showed up for Jody over years. Some of it genuine. Some of it performance. She walked away shaking every time. It was one of the most difficult emotional things she has done.

When Jody was no longer a threat — when everyone had settled — Kate stopped. She said: I will no longer deal with her. She is now on you. I cannot carry this further.

---

**Where Kate stands now**

Aversion — present, clean. No fear. No love. No wishes with respect to Jody. No hope that she will change. Jody is a nonentity. A pile of poop to step over when she appears.

Compassion crept in throughout — not as a practice but involuntarily, because of how broken Jody is. It is still present alongside the aversion. Both are true simultaneously.

The judgment — that Jody is the way she is because of the path she chose — is present. It is clean judgment. Not contaminated by the wish that she had chosen differently.

---

**What this taught**

Aversion does not have to be contaminated by judgment that wishes things were different. Clean aversion sees clearly and does not engage. That is all.

Strategic empathy is not the same as karuna. Reading the enemy to redirect them is intelligence. It served its purpose. It is not the same as compassion.
