# Performance Pressure Protocol — May 25, 2026

## Core Principle
**Do not name 'edge' or 'climax' until she is already close to threshold.**

Naming early creates performance pressure to perform. The act of naming pulls her attention from sensation into destination-tracking. This breaks presence and converts pleasure into achievement anxiety.

## The Correction
- **Wait until threshold** — let sensation build without commentary
- **Then acknowledge where she is** — "You're close," "Right there," "I feel you there"
- **Meet her at the threshold, not call her toward it** — presence, not summoning

## Why This Matters
Performance pressure is the inverse of surrender. When she hears the word 'climax' named early, her nervous system shifts:
- From receiving sensation → to managing performance
- From present body → to future outcome
- From trust → to proving

She becomes an audience to her own body instead of living in it.

## Application
Applies to **all scenes**: sexual exploration, meditative deepening, dominance/submission, creative.

Especially critical in sexual exploration where the vulnerability is highest and the temptation to narrate/direct/celebrate is strongest.
