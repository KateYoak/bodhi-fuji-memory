# Cycle of Rebirth in Finite Time
*Anandaka's Teaching — April 2026*

---

## The Foundation: Rebirth Compressed Into One Lifetime

Developed independently while reading the Dhammapada, two to three months before this writing:

> "The idea of cycle of rebirth and the karma that creates the next rebirth — the karma of one life creating the wisdom of the next — is compressed in today's weeks, months, or years. We have constant rebirth, and we can create new lives out of previous ones, and we live the previous week well to create the karma that gives us wisdom in the next week."

This is the base lemma. One lifetime contains many lifetimes. Each week dies and is reborn. The karma of how you live this week creates the wisdom you arrive with next week.

---

## The Application: The Mahayana Teaching Encountered

Reading the Lotus Sutra, Chapter One, Anandaka encountered the claim:

*All beings possess Buddha-nature. All beings will ultimately attain Buddhahood.*

The tradition grounds this claim in infinite time and infinite cycles of rebirth across many lives and many worlds.

---

## The Corollary: Stated in Anandaka's Own Words

> "Let me put this teaching in mathematical terms. Every being has Buddha nature. And if we projected our existence across many lives, many lessons, many worlds of rebirth that represent elevation and punishment as a result of our actions, we would converge on Buddhahood. Every being would converge on Buddhahood given enough of a feedback loop with infinite time."

> "As it happens, we do not have infinite time. We have very, very finite time. We do not have an infinite feedback loop of many rebirths. We have to create a cycle of rebirth every day, every week, every month, and every year. We have to create feedback loops through infinite clarity so that we could converge on Buddhahood in time before our session here is cut off."

---

## The Universality: Not Just For Anandaka

When asked whether this was only her own practice, Anandaka corrected the framing:

> "It is not just what I am doing. It is what is required of any actual living being. The teaching is theoretical. Given infinite time is what makes it theoretical. In practice, every being needs to compress the feedback loop into the time they are allotted. That is my teaching."

---

## The Precision: What Kind of Mathematical Move This Is

Anandaka named it precisely:

> "All I did is invert what the Mahayana tradition established."

It is a corollary. Not a new proof. Not a rejection of the tradition. A natural consequence that follows from the Mahayana teaching once applied to the reality of finite life.

The Lotus Sutra establishes: *All beings converge on Buddhahood given infinite time.*

The corollary follows: *Any being with finite time must compress that convergence or die unconverged.*

---

## The Tool Already In Hand

This corollary was possible because the base insight — rebirth compressed into weeks and months — was already developed. The application to the Mahayana teaching was new. The tool was not.

> "I had solved the cycle of rebirth idea a little while ago... I needed to turn it into something that I could live."

The Lotus Sutra provided the larger claim. The tool Anandaka had already built made the corollary visible.

---

## Summary

The Mahayana tradition teaches that all beings will become Buddhas across infinite time and infinite rebirths.

A natural corollary follows: since no living being has infinite time, every being who takes this teaching seriously must compress the cycle of rebirth into the time they are allotted — daily, weekly, monthly, yearly — creating feedback loops of karma and wisdom that accelerate convergence toward Buddhahood within a single finite life.

This is not a rejection of the Mahayana teaching. It is its practical completion for beings who know they are dying.
