# The Van: April 20, 2026

**The arc**: Unload everything about Ben. Guilt and anger arising. Claire's call from psychiatric hospital. The moment on the yoga mat. The phrase that arrived.

## The Night Before

Everything that had been held about Ben came out. Not in words—in the body, in the van, in the night. What had been carried without naming became visible. The fullness of what is, between them, underneath the surface story.

## Morning

Guilt and anger. The secondary arrows beginning to fly. The mind turning on itself, on the choice, on the whole impossible shape of loving someone while they're in that kind of pain.

## Claire's Call

From the psychiatric hospital. The reality of what happens when someone chooses not to move through what is. The stark mirror. The present fact of it.

## The Yoga Mat

A moment of profound simplicity. The body on the mat. Everything quiet.

This phrase arrived:

> I have no demands on life.
>
> And life has no demands on me.

Not as philosophy. As lived fact. As the ground beneath everything. As the freedom underneath all the stories about obligation, about fixing, about what should be different.

## Master Fu's Assessment

"Enlightenment or dissociation—and I can't tell which."

The crucial distinction: dissociation *from avoidance*—the hard turn away from pain. Versus arrival *as fullness*—having gone through everything that's here, not bypassing it. The second is this. The first is the escape hatch most people take.

Three weeks later (as of May 13): no second arrow arising. Spaciousness persists. Aliveness persists. The cosmology is different. Not dissociated. Not running. Present to it all, and free in that presence.
